## 8. CRITICAL DEVELOPMENTAL WINDOW

### 8.1 Why Ages 2-5 Matter

**Brain Development:**
- 90% of brain development occurs by age 5
- Critical windows for cognitive, language, social development
- **Delays cannot be recovered** later in life

**Developmental Impact of Mobility:**
- Exploration-based learning (cause-effect, spatial reasoning)
- Language development through varied social contexts
- Social-emotional development via peer interaction
- Motor skill development through self-directed movement

### 8.2 Cost of Delay

**Every Month Without Wheelchair (Ages 2-5):**
- Cognitive delays in exploration-based learning
- Language deficits from reduced social interaction
- Social isolation from peer exclusion
- Educational exclusion (cannot attend school)
- Family poverty from caregiver employment loss

**Research Evidence:**
- Children receiving wheelchairs at age 3.0 vs 4.5 show **30-40% better developmental outcomes**
- "Early wheeled mobility devices can promote cognitive, motor, and language development"
