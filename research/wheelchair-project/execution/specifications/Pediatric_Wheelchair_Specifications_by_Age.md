# Children's Wheelchair Sizes: A Quick Guide

**For Rotarians** | January 2026

---

## How Wheelchair Sizing Works

Like clothing, wheelchairs are sized by **seat width** — how wide the seat is. A child needs a chair that fits their body: too big and they slide around; too small and they won't fit comfortably.

---

## Size Comparison by Age

| | Ages 2-5 | Ages 6-12 | Ages 13-18 |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Seat Width** | 10-12" | 12-14" | 14-18" |
| **Similar to...** | Toddler car seat | Kitchen chair | Adult chair |
| **Wheel Size** | 18-20" (small) | 20-22" (medium) | 22-24" (full) |
| **Weight Limit** | 60 kg / 130 lbs | 80 kg / 175 lbs | 115 kg / 250 lbs |
| **Lasts** | 2-3 years | 2-3 years | 3-5 years |

---

## What Each Age Group Needs

### Toddlers (2-5 years)
The smallest chairs, for preschool-age children. **This is the most critical age** — 90% of brain development happens by age 5, so early mobility matters enormously.

**Must have:** Anti-tip bars (toddlers tip easily), safety belt, bright colors
**Challenge:** Most manufacturers don't make sizes this small — we need custom orders

### School Age (6-12 years)
Children who need to get to school, fit through doorways, and roll up to desks.

**Must have:** Foldable frame, flip-up armrests, swing-away footrests
**Good news:** Standard children's models (like Brother Medical's BME4617K) fit this group

### Teenagers (13-18 years)
Approaching adult size. Appearance matters — they want modern-looking chairs, not medical equipment.

**Must have:** Hand rims for self-propulsion, durable frame, modern look
**Note:** Larger teens can transition to adult wheelchair models

---

## Children with Cerebral Palsy (43% of our need)

CP children need **positioning wheelchairs** with extra support — a basic chair won't work:
- Head rests and trunk supports
- Chest straps and hip belts
- Reclining or tilting seats

These cost more ($150-250 vs $35-50) but are essential.
