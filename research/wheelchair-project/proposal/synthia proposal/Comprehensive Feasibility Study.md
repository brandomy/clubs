**Rotary Club Georgetown Wheelchair Project**

**Comprehensive Feasibility Study**  

**Addressing Mobility Gaps in Penang's Public Hospitals & Underserved Communities**


**1.Executive Summary**

 

The Rotary Club Georgetown Penang Wheelchair Project addresses a critical, often-overlooked need for appropriate mobility devices in Penang, Malaysia. Despite Penang's status as high-income state, “significant disparities persist” in access to wheelchairs for low-income communities, elderly populations, and patients within the public healthcare system. An estimated 8,000+ individuals in Penang lack access to functional, well-fitted wheelchairs, leading to preventable health complications, social exclusion, and economic hardship. This project will procure and distribute 1000 specialised wheelchairs, establish maintenance systems, and train healthcare workers – directly aligning with Malaysia's National Policy for Persons with Disabilities and Penang's vision for inclusive development. We seek RM 1,200,000 (approx. $250,000 USD) to implement this high-impact, sustainable initiative.


**2\. Problem Statement: Penang's Hidden Mobility Crisis**  

**2.1. Scale of Need**  

Public Hospitals: Penang's public hospitals (e.g., Hospital Pulau Pinang, Hospital Seberang Jaya) serve over 1.5 million residents. 

The report:  

40% shortage of appropriate wheelchairs (Ministry of Health Malaysia, 2023).  

  \- High reliance on donated, ill-fitting, or broken chairs leading to pressure injuries and falls.  

  \- Disproportionate impact on elderly patients (60+ years \= 15% of Penang's population).  

**Underserved Communities**:  

  \- Low-Income Urban Areas: Flats in Jelutong, Air Itam, and Perai house families earning \< RM 2,000/month. A basic wheelchair costs 1-3 months' wages.  

  \- Rural Villages: Communities in Balik Pulau and south Seberang Perai lack transport to access hospitals or repair services.  

  **\- Key Groups Affected:**  

    \- Elderly with stroke, arthritis, or fall-related injuries (aging population growing 3.1% annually).  

    \- Children with cerebral palsy or developmental disabilities.  

    \- Diabetic amputees (Penang has Malaysia's 2nd highest diabetes prevalence at 22.6%).  

    \- Industrial accident victims (manufacturing sector employs 30% of Penang's workforce).


**2.2. Consequences of Inaction**

Health: Pressure ulcers, muscle contractures, respiratory infections, and reduced life expectancy.  

Economic:  85% of disabled low-income adults are unemployed, trapping families in intergenerational poverty.  

Social:  60% report inability to attend school, work, or religious activities; high rates of depression.  

System Strain:  Prolonged hospital stays due to lack of safe discharge mobility aids, increasing bed shortages.


**3\. Project Solution: Rotary's Targeted Intervention** 

3.1. Objectives 

1\. Distribute 1000 appropriate wheelchairs (adult, paediatric, bariatric, all-terrain) to public hospitals and community hubs.  

2\. Train 150+ healthcare workers (nurses, physiotherapists) and 50 community volunteers in assessment, fitting, and basic maintenance.  

3\. Establish 3 wheelchair repair hubs with local technicians for sustainability.  

4\. Partner with Penang State Council for Persons with Disabilities to advocate for policy integration.

\- Impact:  85% of disabled poor rely on shared/broken wheelchairs or makeshift solutions (JKM Outreach Report).
