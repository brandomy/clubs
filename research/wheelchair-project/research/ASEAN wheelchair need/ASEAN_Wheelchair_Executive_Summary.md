# Part 1: Narrative Summary

## Key Finding

**Children ages 2-5 are the most critically underserved cohort across ASEAN, with 85-97% lacking wheelchair access during the developmental window when 90% of brain architecture forms.** This gap is not accidental—it results from healthcare systems designed for adults, rapid-growth economics that make pediatric wheelchairs 3-4× costlier over a lifetime, and young children's inability to self-advocate. Every month of delay causes permanent cognitive, language, and social deficits.

## Context

HuaQiao Foundation's $47.50 landed cost model creates transformative value against regional retail prices of $200-400. In Myanmar, a pediatric wheelchair costs 545% of monthly low-income household income; in Vietnam, 344%. The foundation's container economics—500 wheelchairs for $24,000—could address 40% of Cambodia's entire pediatric wheelchair need in one shipment. This research identifies where that container creates maximum developmental impact.

## Top Recommendations (Prioritized)

1. **Target age 2-5 exclusively in Phase 1** — Specify adjustable frames for toddler sizing to Chinese manufacturers. This cohort has 95% gap rates versus 40-60% for teenagers, and intervention ROI is highest due to brain development window.

2. **Phase 1 Countries (0-6 months): Indonesia → Philippines → Vietnam**
   - **Indonesia**: Partner with UCPRUK (WHO-certified) and Ministry of Social Affairs "Indonesia Walks" program. Largest absolute need (700K-1.6M children).
   - **Philippines**: Engage Tahanang Walang Hagdanan—only domestic wheelchair manufacturer with LDS Charities distribution network.
   - **Vietnam**: Connect with VNAH for government relationships and 128,000-device track record.

3. **Phase 2 Countries (6-12 months): Cambodia → Laos → Thailand rural**
   - Cambodia via Exceed Worldwide (route through Thailand's Laem Chabang port)
   - Laos via COPE (acute need after January 2025 USAID cuts)
   - Thailand rural populations outside Bangkok via RICD network

4. **Establish dual-hub logistics**: Port Klang (Malaysia) for Maritime ASEAN; Laem Chabang (Thailand) for Mainland ASEAN. All qualify for 0% ATIGA duty with Form D Certificate.

5. **Apply for Rotary Global Grant**: District 3310 (Singapore) can provide $30,000+ matching funds.

## Critical Caveats

| Issue | Impact on Decision |
|-------|-------------------|
| No ASEAN country maintains pediatric wheelchair registries by age | Gap percentages are extrapolated from global patterns—actual 2-5 year need may be higher |
| Indonesia range is 700K-1.6M (2.3× variance) | Recommend pilot program before scaling |
| Myanmar data unreliable post-2021 coup | Deprioritize despite extreme need |
| Wait time data systematically uncollected | Cannot verify "8-18 month" figures beyond Malaysia |

## Major Data Gaps

- No pediatric wheelchair registries by age cohort in any ASEAN country
- Wait time data systematically uncollected (Malaysia 8-18 months is only verified figure)
- Myanmar post-2021 data unreliable
- Rural vs urban disparities undocumented
- Gender disparities in provision unmeasured
- Quality assessment of provided wheelchairs does not exist
