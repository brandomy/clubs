# Committee Chair Responsibilities: Stonyhurst Interact Club Launch

## Your Core Accountability

As Youth Service Committee Chair, you're responsible for:
- Ensuring all youth protection policies are followed (non-negotiable)
- Recruiting and vetting qualified advisors
- Coordinating with Stonyhurst administration
- Reporting club formation to Rotary International
- Ongoing oversight of club operations

**Bottom line:** You're personally accountable for youth safety and compliance.

---

## Critical First Steps

### 1. Recruit Advisors

**MANDATORY:**
- One Stonyhurst faculty member (primary advisor)

**RECOMMENDED:**
- 2-3 Georgetown Rotarians as co-advisors (shared workload)

### 2. Complete Volunteer Clearance (ALL advisors)

**Required before any youth contact:**
1. Written volunteer application
2. Background check + reference checks + interview
3. Youth Protection training (my.rotary.org → Learning Center)
4. Report primary advisor to RI by July 1

**Timeline:** Allow 4-6 weeks minimum for clearance process.

### 3. Coordinate with Stonyhurst

**Your meetings with school admin:**
- Present Interact program overview
- Identify faculty advisor candidate
- Establish meeting schedule (2x monthly typical)
- Clarify school policy requirements
- Define liability/insurance coverage
- Set communication protocols

**Get in writing:**
- School approval for club formation
- Meeting space allocation
- Faculty advisor assignment
- Any school-specific requirements

---

## Ongoing Oversight Responsibilities

**Youth Protection:**
- Never allow 1 adult alone with 1 youth
- Monitor meetings (unannounced visits occasionally)
- Maintain documentation of all training/screening
- Immediate reporting of any concerns to District YPO

**Club Operations:**
- Ensure 2 annual service projects (1 community + 1 international)
- Support but don't control (students lead, advisors facilitate)
- Maintain records and financial oversight
- Connect club to district Interact network

**Annual Requirements:**
- Recertify all advisors
- Report to RI by July 1
- Submit club activity reports

## Timeline for Launch

**Months 1-2:**
- Recruit advisors from Georgetown
- Coordinate with Stonyhurst for faculty advisor
- Initiate background screening for all

**Month 3:**
- Complete all volunteer clearance
- Finalize school agreement
- Set meeting schedule

**Month 4:**
- Student recruitment at Stonyhurst
- Draft club constitution/bylaws
- Elect student officers

**Month 5:**
- Submit club certification to RI
- Receive charter (4-6 weeks)
- Plan charter ceremony

**Month 6:**
- Charter ceremony
- First service project planning

---

## Critical Don'ts

**Never:**
- Allow volunteers to work with youth before clearance complete
- Skip background checks "because we know them"
- Allow 1-on-1 adult-youth situations
- Investigate abuse allegations yourself (report to authorities)
- Compromise safety for program convenience

---

## When to Escalate

**Contact District Youth Protection Officer immediately if:**
- Any volunteer fails background check
- Abuse/harassment alleged or suspected
- Policy violations observed
- Unclear about any safety requirement
- School raises concerns

**Contact District Interact Chair for:**
- Program guidance
- Connecting to other clubs
- Best practices
- Resources

---

## Success Metrics

**You've succeeded when:**
- All advisors properly cleared and trained
- School administration fully supportive
- Students leading their own meetings/projects
- Zero safety incidents
- Club meets RI standards for recognition

---

## Bottom Line

Your job is creating the safe, compliant framework that allows students to lead and serve. You're not running the club - you're ensuring the adults who support it are qualified and the environment is safe.

**If ever uncertain: pause, consult District YPO, document everything.**
