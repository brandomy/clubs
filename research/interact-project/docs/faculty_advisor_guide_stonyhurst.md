# Faculty Advisor Guide: Stonyhurst Interact Club

## Your Role

As faculty advisor, you ensure the club complies with school policies while supporting student leadership. You're the bridge between Rotary and Stonyhurst.

**Core principle:** Students lead, you facilitate.

---

## Before You Start

**Mandatory clearance (coordinated by Rotary Georgetown):**
1. Complete volunteer application + background check
2. Complete online Youth Protection training (30-40 min at my.rotary.org)
3. Interview with Youth Protection Officer

**Timeline:** 4-6 weeks. No youth contact until complete.

---

## Your Key Responsibilities

**Safety & Compliance:**
- Attend all club meetings (typically 2x monthly)
- Never allow 1 adult alone with 1 youth (two-deep leadership)
- Ensure school policies followed
- Report any concerns immediately

**Student Support:**
- Guide meeting structure (students run meetings)
- Support service project planning (students choose and lead)
- Help with logistics (room booking, permissions, equipment)
- Connect students to resources

**Rotary Connection:**
- Coordinate with Georgetown Rotarian co-advisors
- Help maintain club records
- Participate in district Interact events

---

## Club Startup Checklist

### Phase 1: Foundation (Month 1-2)
- [ ] Complete clearance requirements
- [ ] Meet with Rotary co-advisors to align
- [ ] Reserve meeting space and schedule
- [ ] Plan student recruitment approach

### Phase 2: Student Recruitment (Month 3)
- [ ] Present Interact to eligible students (ages 12-18)
- [ ] Distribute information about program
- [ ] Collect student interest forms
- [ ] Get parent permissions for participation

### Phase 3: Club Formation (Month 4)
- [ ] Hold first organizational meeting
- [ ] Students draft club constitution/bylaws
- [ ] Elect student officers (president, VP, secretary, treasurer)
- [ ] Set regular meeting schedule

### Phase 4: Official Charter (Month 5)
- [ ] Submit certification form to Rotary International
- [ ] Receive official charter (4-6 weeks)
- [ ] Plan charter ceremony
- [ ] Begin service project planning

---

## Running First Few Meetings

### Meeting 1: Introduction (30-45 min)
1. Welcome & introductions
2. Explain Interact: youth-led service club
3. Show connection to Rotary Georgetown
4. Discuss benefits: leadership, service, global network
5. Q&A
6. Next steps: return interest forms

### Meeting 2: Organization (45-60 min)
1. Ice breaker activity
2. Review Interact requirements:
   - 2 service projects/year (1 community + 1 international)
   - Meet 2x monthly minimum
   - Student-led with adult guidance
3. Brainstorm club goals and interests
4. Explain officer roles
5. Set date for elections

### Meeting 3: Elections & Planning (60 min)
1. Hold officer elections
2. New officers present their goals
3. Brainstorm first service project ideas
4. Form project committees
5. Set next meeting agenda

### Meeting 4+: Regular Operations (45-60 min)
**Standard agenda:**
- Call to order (student president)
- Announcements
- Committee updates
- Project planning/discussion
- Action items for next meeting
- Adjournment

**Your role:** Observe, support, intervene only for safety/policy

---

## Key Reminders

**Students lead:**
- They choose projects
- They run meetings
- They make decisions
- You guide, don't control

**Two service projects annually:**
- One benefits local community/school
- One promotes international understanding
- Students plan and execute

**Safety rules:**
- Always 2+ adults OR 2+ students present
- Never transport single student without written parent permission
- Any concerns → report immediately to you and Rotary

**Documentation:**
- Keep attendance records
- Track service hours
- Maintain parent permissions
- Document any incidents

---

## When Things Go Wrong

**Student conflict:** Help them resolve it themselves first, mediate if needed

**Project not going well:** Let them learn from mistakes (unless safety issue)

**Low attendance:** Work with student officers on engagement strategies

**Safety concern:** Report immediately to school admin and Rotary Georgetown
