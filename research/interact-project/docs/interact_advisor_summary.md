# Interact Advisor: Roles & Responsibilities Summary

## Who Can Be an Interact Advisor

**Eligible Adults:**
- School faculty members (required for school-based clubs)
- Rotary Club members
- Rotaract Club members
- Parents or community volunteers

**For School-Based Clubs (like Stonyhurst):**
- **MANDATORY:** At least one advisor must be a school faculty member
- **OPTIONAL:** Additional Rotarian co-advisors can support the faculty advisor

---

## Advisor Structure Options

**Single Faculty Advisor:**
- One teacher serves as primary advisor
- Rotarians support from sponsor club side

**Co-Advisor Model (Recommended):**
- Primary: Faculty member (meets school requirements)
- Co-Advisors: 2-3 Rotarians from Georgetown
- Shared responsibilities, better coverage

---

## Mandatory Requirements

### 1. Youth Protection Compliance
- **Complete Rotary youth protection training** (online course)
- **Pass background screening/vetting** (coordinated by sponsor club)
- **Annual recertification** required

### 2. Meeting Attendance
- **Attend ALL Interact club meetings** (typically twice monthly)
- Ensure safe environment and policy compliance

### 3. Official Reporting
- Faculty advisor name must be reported to Rotary International annually (by July 1)
- Ensures club remains officially active

---

## Core Responsibilities

### Day-to-Day Club Operations
- Guide (not control) club meetings and activities
- Oversee officer elections for fairness
- Maintain club records and event calendar
- Help manage club funds with sponsor club oversight
- Ensure school policies are followed (faculty advisor)

### Service Project Support
- Support 2 annual service projects (1 community + 1 international)
- Facilitate project planning (not project doing)
- Coordinate logistics, equipment, transportation
- Let students lead; advisor guides

### Rotary Connection
- Liaise with sponsor Rotary club (Georgetown)
- Coordinate with district Interact chair
- Connect students to leadership training (RYLA, district events)
- Promote club to school and community

### Student Development
- Create safe space for student voice and leadership
- Support students through challenges and mistakes
- Help resolve conflicts constructively
- Recognize and celebrate accomplishments

---

## Faculty Advisor Unique Role

**School Compliance:**
- Ensure club meets school extracurricular requirements
- Maintain academic standards for members
- Follow school internet/communication policies
- Coordinate with school administration

**Accessibility:**
- Primary contact for students during school hours
- Present at school-based activities
- Bridge between Rotary and school culture

---

## Rotarian Co-Advisor Support

**What Rotarians Bring:**
- Rotary knowledge and network connections
- Professional mentorship and expertise
- Financial/logistical support coordination
- Coverage when faculty advisor unavailable
- Link to district and international Rotary

**Time Commitment:**
- Attend club meetings (or alternate with co-advisors)
- Support major service projects
- Help with leadership training coordination
- 4-6 hours monthly typical

---

## Critical Notes for Committee

1. **Multiple advisors recommended** - reduces burden, provides continuity
2. **Faculty member is non-negotiable** for school-based clubs
3. **Youth protection training is mandatory** - no exceptions
4. **Students lead, advisors facilitate** - not a teacher-led club
5. **Annual commitment preferred** but multi-year creates stability
