# Youth Protection Essentials for Committee Chair

## Chair's Core Duty

You're personally responsible for ensuring no volunteer works with youth until ALL requirements complete and all policies followed.

---

## 4 Mandatory Steps (No Exceptions)

**Before ANY volunteer works with youth:**

1. **Written application** + criminal history disclosure
2. **Background check** + 2-3 reference checks + interview
3. **Youth Protection training** (online, 30-40 min, renew every 3 years)
4. **Report primary advisor to RI** (annually by July 1)

---

## Non-Negotiable Rules

**Supervision:**
- Never 1 adult alone with 1 youth
- Always: 2+ adults with 1 youth, OR 1 adult with 2+ youth
- Applies to ALL activities: meetings, projects, transportation

**Transportation:**
- Never transport single youth without express written parent permission

**Overnight:**
- Written parent permission required
- Adults never shower/use toilets with youth

---

## Immediate Disqualification

**Permanently banned from ANY Rotary youth work:**
- Anyone with history of sexual abuse/harassment of youth
- Anyone convicted of abuse-related crimes

---

## If Abuse Alleged or Suspected

**Your immediate steps:**
1. Report to police/child protective services FIRST
2. Contact District Youth Protection Officer
3. Remove accused from youth contact immediately
4. Document everything

**Never investigate yourself - that's law enforcement's job.**

---

## Required Documentation

**Must maintain secure records:**
- All volunteer screening/training certificates
- Monitoring visit notes
- Any incidents/allegations
- List of prohibited persons

**Records transfer to next chair.**

---

## Monitoring Required

- Unannounced meeting visits
- Verify 2-deep leadership compliance
- Watch for red flags: gifts to individuals, private time-seeking, inappropriate contact
- Document observations

---

## Consequences of Non-Compliance

- Club charter termination
- Program suspension
- Personal liability
- Criminal charges if negligence leads to abuse
