# Pitchmasters Visual Charter

## PURPOSE

Pitchmasters maintains a constrained visual system to prevent stylistic drift and ensure long-term coherence across all visual materials. Visuals exist to support thinking, not to decorate content. This charter enforces discipline in metaphor selection, composition, and execution so that images produced by different people or tools remain recognizably part of the same system.

---

## VISUAL PHILOSOPHY

All Pitchmasters visuals are governed by the following principles:

- **Editorial over illustrative.** Images clarify concepts; they do not narrate scenarios.
- **Signal over noise.** Every element must serve a clear function. Decorative elements are prohibited.
- **Restraint over expressiveness.** Visuals prioritize calm intelligence over emotional appeal.
- **Meaning over aesthetics.** Form follows the requirements of the metaphor, not current design trends.
- **Durability over novelty.** Images must remain legible and appropriate for a minimum of five years.

---

## COMPOSITION RULES (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

- Canvas MUST be square (1:1 ratio) or horizontal (16:9 or 4:3). Vertical formats are prohibited.
- Each image MUST contain no more than three distinct visual elements. A distinct visual element is defined as a form that carries independent semantic meaning within the metaphor.
- Negative space MUST occupy at least 40% of the canvas.
- Central focus MUST be a single object or form. Multiple focal points are prohibited.
- Backgrounds MUST be flat color fields or minimal gradients. Textures, patterns, and photographic backgrounds are prohibited.
- All elements MUST be rendered in clean geometric forms or simple line work. Organic, hand-drawn, or textured rendering styles are prohibited.

---

## METAPHOR USAGE RULES

- Each image MUST represent exactly one canonical metaphor from the approved system: Compression, Calibration, Threshold, Structure, Iteration, or Bearing.
- Metaphors MUST NOT be combined within a single image.
- Metaphors MUST be represented abstractly. Literal depictions (meetings, stages, people speaking, audience members, presentation screens) are prohibited.
- If a metaphor cannot be represented without depicting human activity, it MUST be rejected and a different metaphor selected.
- Metaphor selection MUST align with meeting theme or content purpose. Alignment MUST be explicit and defensible. If the metaphor cannot be justified in one sentence, it is misaligned.

---

## COLOR DISCIPLINE

- Each image MUST use no more than three colors total: one background, one primary element, one accent (optional).
- Color selection MUST prioritize contrast and legibility over brand alignment.
- When producing multiple images for a single context (meeting series, document set), background color MUST remain consistent across all images.
- Gradients, when used, MUST transition between two specified colors only. Multi-stop gradients are prohibited.
- Color MUST NOT be selected for emotional effect. Color exists solely to establish visual hierarchy and contrast.

---

## FORBIDDEN ELEMENTS (HARD REJECTION LIST)

The following elements immediately disqualify an image:

- People (illustrated, photographic, or silhouetted)
- Faces or facial features
- Hands or gestures
- Speech bubbles, text balloons, or dialogue indicators
- Presentation screens, projectors, or display devices
- Stages, podiums, or platforms
- Audiences, crowds, or groups
- Charts, graphs, or data visualizations
- Lightbulbs, rockets, or innovation clichés
- Arrows indicating direction or flow (except as static, minimal structural elements integral to an approved metaphor)
- Expressive or dynamic motion lines
- Photographic imagery or photorealistic rendering
- Decorative borders, frames, or ornamental elements

---

## TONE CHECK (FINAL GATE)

Before approving any image, answer the following questions. All answers must be "yes."

1. **Calmness:** Could this image appear in a technical journal or graduate-level textbook without seeming out of place?
2. **Clarity:** Can the visual metaphor be understood without accompanying text or context?
3. **Durability:** Will this image remain appropriate and legible in five years, regardless of design trends?
4. **Maturity:** Would a seasoned founder view this as a thinking tool rather than marketing material?
5. **Restraint:** If one element were removed, would the image lose essential meaning? If not, remove it.

If any answer is "no," the image MUST be revised or rejected.

---

**This charter is non-negotiable. Exceptions require written justification and approval from club leadership.**
