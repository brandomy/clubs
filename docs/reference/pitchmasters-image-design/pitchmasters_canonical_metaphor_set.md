# Pitchmasters Canonical Metaphor Set

**Authoritative / Locked**

The Pitchmasters visual system is built on a closed set of canonical metaphors. These metaphors define the only conceptual territory visuals may occupy.

Each image MUST map to exactly one metaphor from this set. No additions or substitutions are permitted without formal review.

---

## 1. Compression

**Metaphor:** Distillation

**Conceptual Domain:** Reduction of complexity into essential signal.

**Scope of Use:**  
Used when the meeting focus involves:
- Pitch refinement
- Message clarity
- Removing excess detail
- Condensing narratives or business models

**Visual Characteristics:**
- Forms that imply concentration or refinement
- Clear reduction from many to one
- No implication of loss; emphasis on potency

---

## 2. Calibration

**Metaphor:** Reference / Measurement

**Conceptual Domain:** Alignment through external feedback.

**Scope of Use:**  
Used when the meeting focus involves:
- Evaluation
- Peer feedback
- Standards of clarity
- Objective improvement

**Visual Characteristics:**
- Reference marks or alignment points
- Symmetry or controlled offset
- Neutral, impartial forms

---

## 3. Threshold

**Metaphor:** Filtered Signal Crossing a Boundary

**Conceptual Domain:** Discrete transition from ambiguity to clarity.

**Scope of Use:**  
Used when the meeting focus involves:
- Breakthrough moments
- Decision points
- Investor readiness
- Message acceptance

**Visual Characteristics:**
- Clear boundary or threshold
- Contrast between pre- and post-transition states
- No dramatization of the crossing

---

## 4. Structure

**Metaphor:** Load-Bearing Framework

**Conceptual Domain:** Communication as foundational support.

**Scope of Use:**  
Used when the meeting focus involves:
- Speech structure
- Argument logic
- Foundational skills
- Long-term capability building

**Visual Characteristics:**
- Stable frameworks
- Clear joints or supports
- Implicit load without collapse

---

## 5. Iteration

**Metaphor:** Upward Spiral

**Conceptual Domain:** Progress through disciplined repetition.

**Scope of Use:**  
Used when the meeting focus involves:
- Practice cycles
- Incremental improvement
- Skill compounding
- Long-term growth

**Visual Characteristics:**
- Ascending spiral or stepped progression
- Even spacing
- Clear direction without urgency

---

## 6. Bearing

**Metaphor:** Azimuth / Directional Reference

**Conceptual Domain:** Orientation under uncertainty.

**Scope of Use:**  
Used when the meeting focus involves:
- Strategic clarity
- Founder mindset
- Navigating ambiguity
- Maintaining direction

**Visual Characteristics:**
- Reference axes or markers
- Balanced, centered compositions
- No destination or path implied

---

## Governance Note

This set is closed by default.

**New metaphors may be proposed only if:**
- They address a clearly unmet conceptual need
- They do not overlap with existing metaphors
- They can be expressed abstractly and durably

**Any addition or modification requires:**
- Written justification
- Approval by club leadership
- Update of the Visual Charter

---

**Together, the Visual Charter and the Canonical Metaphor Set form a complete, enforceable system:**
- Meaning is fixed
- Execution is constrained
- Cohesion is guaranteed

This is no longer a "design preference." It is Pitchmasters visual infrastructure.
